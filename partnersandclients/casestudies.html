Blizzcon 2010

The annual event held by Blizzard attracts tens of thousands fans every year to Anaheim. Blizzard and DirecTV partnered with RayV to provide a live broadcast of the event to the hundreds of thousands of fans in 150 countries who wanted to be part of the event but could not physically attend. The event included five stages covering tournaments, shows, Q&A’s sessions, competitions, announcementand more. It is considered the prime event of the gaming world every year.

Blizzcon 2010 served over 550,000 unique viewers watching over 100,000,000 minutes of online streaming in a single weekend, The premium package subscription cost $40 and included HD streaming of the entire event plus a special “pet code”

AB Groupe launched one on the first true French Over The Top initiatives, providing its full range of TV channels as well as syndicated channels from affiliates over the Internet within France and to the French Expat Community. A total of 33 channels.

Together, we created a new Over The Top offering based on a network of 18 sites with different themes. This unique approach allows users to choose between accessing a dedicated website for a vertical topic or opt in for a bundled offering. America's Cup

The city of Valencia, Spain was the host city for the 32nd Americas Cup held in 2007. After much dispute between the teams about the rules for staging the next event, Valencia was awarded all media rights one month before the 33rd Americas Cup held in February, 2010. With little time to organize traditional broadcast distribution, the organizers decided to implement a broadband-led strategy, offering live streaming of the races to audiences worldwide for free. Understanding that it had insufficient time to capitalize financially on the rights granted to it, Valencia’s mission was to set-up an infrastructure capable of managing a global broadband transmission in a very short time frame while having as many users as possible watching the live coverage of the race to serve as marketing and promotion vehicles for the next edition of the event.

RayV responded within a two-week period to the requests of the city of Valencia. RayV Set up a real-time fully redundant VHO within the city’s media center, and coverage of the two live races as well as press conferences and other ancillary content were streamed to over 500,000 users worldwide via the Americas Cup website and its syndication partners (including YouTube).

Dan Patrick

Dan Patrick’s popular radio show is now available as a Live HDTV show, with multiple live cameras capturing the action straight from the radio station.

The show is available live everyday on DirecTV Channel 101, and it is now available nationwide to all fans including those who are not DirecTV subscribers.

Together with RayV, DirecTV created a unique Over The Top offering allowing anyone to watch the show live in HD over the Internet. The users requesting the feed would be authenticated using their subscriber information -- those who are DirecTV subscribers receive the feed for free and others pay $7.99 per month.
